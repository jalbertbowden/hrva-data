21st Century Curriculum & Instruction
I will now highlight a few areas of focus that are still in process.

The initiative to increase building principals’ instructional leadership capacity continues with an emphasis on improving and expanding the types of assessments used by teachers, by infusing innovative instructional strategies and supportive instructional technologies into teacher’s pedagogy.  Next year, assistant principals will be included in these training opportunities so that instructional staff receive a clear and consistent message regarding the delivery of rigorous and relevant instruction.

A plan is currently being developed to implement Microsoft Outlook as a student email system for all high school students starting in the fall semester.

Additionally, a list of “look-fors” regarding student engagement developed by teachers noted for their ability to engage students in academic work and students from across the division will be finalized early next school year.

Work also continues on a program that recognizes schools that develop exemplary wellness activities aimed at addressing health literacy within their communities. This program, which will emphasize fitness, nutrition, and parent and community partnerships, provides incentives and support for schools that promote health and fitness through systematic initiatives such as the program at Luxford Elementary School, which was highlighted during a recent board workshop.
